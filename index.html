Polar Bear Info

Polar Bear

Scientific Name: Ursus maritimus

Diet and Hunting

  • Main Diet: They are carnivores, primarily feeding on seals, especially the ringed seal (Pusa hispida) and the bearded seal (Erignathus barbatus).
  • Hunting Method: Polar bears are agile hunters in extreme conditions. One of their main techniques is waiting motionless near breathing holes used by seals in the ice. When a seal emerges to breathe, the bear swiftly captures it.
  • Occasionally Eats: Carrion, fish, birds, eggs, and even vegetation when food is scarce.
  • Fasting: In summer, when the ice melts, they can go months without eating, surviving on their fat reserves.

Physical Characteristics

  • Size: Males measure between 2.4 and 3 meters in length, while females are smaller, ranging from 1.8 to 2.4 meters.
  • Weight: Males weigh between 350 and 700 kg, but some exceed 800 kg. Females weigh between 150 and 400 kg.
  • Fur: Their fur appears white but is actually transparent, reflecting light. Beneath their fur, their skin is black, helping them absorb heat.
  • Fat Layer: They have a thick fat layer up to 10 cm, insulating them from extreme cold.
  • Paws: Large and wide, with sharp claws for walking on ice and catching prey. Their paw pads have tiny bumps that prevent slipping on icy surfaces.
  • Hearing and Smell: They have a highly developed sense of smell, capable of detecting prey from miles away.
  • Vision: Similar to humans, with good night vision to see in the Arctic winter darkness.

Fun Facts

  • Their skin is black, but their fur reflects light, making them appear white.
  • They can smell a seal over 1.5 km away and beneath thick ice.
  • Their bite is as strong as that of a great white shark.
  • They are the only bears considered marine mammals since they depend on the sea for food.
  • Did you know?
    The polar bear is the only bear against which no survival strategy works: whether you run away, hide or fight, it will see you as prey and due to the difficulty it has to get food, it will not miss opportunities.

Habitat

  • Geographic Location: The polar bear lives in the Arctic Circle, in countries such as Canada, Greenland, Norway, Russia, and the United States (Alaska).
  • Climate and Conditions: It lives in an extremely cold environment, with sub-zero temperatures, strong winds, and frozen seas.
  • Sea Ice: Its main habitat is sea ice, where it hunts seals, its main source of food.
  • Adaptations to the Environment: It has a thick coat, a layer of insulating fat, and wide legs that allow it to walk on ice and swim long distances.
  • Threats: Climate change is reducing sea ice, making hunting and survival difficult.
